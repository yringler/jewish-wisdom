Yechida w/name and w/out. W/: A"K, image of essence which can relate to world. W/out name: before tzimtzum, yachid. Included in this etzem is that could be keser and malchus, aprox echad and kadmon. That could have an abstract desire and a more set application of that. Up there, an ability.

Yosher of A"K: idea of moshiach b'poel. Iggulim of A"K{/of this and next chapter} is more general idea, e.g. dirah b'tachtonim.

We call over here the first circle reishis hakav, and the first bit reshis hakav ha'heyuli, reshis could be either. First circle = highest priority of the kav.
 שנק'
This first circle.
 מקיף כללי לכללות כל הד' עולמות דאבי"ע שמקיף אותם בשוה
Diffrences between diffrent parts of implementing the desire aren't inherently recognized, at this point they're all seen as being of the same importance.
The kav makes a structure of priorities, drawing one circle inside that one etc, one at a time, from most to least important. Which is why we say that there isn't a hishtalshilus in ratzon, really even the tiniest thing is streight from the etzem, just that kav makes it that comes out later and its seen as being less important.
Example of this equaltiy of desire: Someone loves his mother and wants to take care of her. So works hard, becomes wealthy - by the time he gets there the supposedly lower desire for money, which was originaly intended to serve the desire to help his mother, takes control, and he's only interested in the money and doesn't want to give to anyone else. How can this happen? Because in the world of desire nothing is inherently higher, priorty is something imposed by the kav.
This is where tohu comes in, because every desire contains the whole etzem, someone can get caught up in that to the exclusion of all else.
In tikkun, theres the idea of "means to an end". In tohu, any of those can become all-important. Deeper taanug in tikkun, develops in a person later. Iggulim/Yosher mokur of tohu/tikkun, to be discussed later.
